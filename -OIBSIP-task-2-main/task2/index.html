Steve Jobs (American businessman)

1955 - 2011

Cofounder of Apple Computer, Inc. (now Apple Inc.), and a charismatic pioneer of the personal computer era


Steve Jobs was an American entrepreneur, computer designer and businessman. He cofounded the Apple company and oversaw the invention of the Apple, iMac and Macintosh computers as well as the iPod, iPhone and iPad. His vision of inexpensive computers designed for regular people helped launch the personal computing industry. Jobs was one of the first entrepreneurs to understand that the personal computer would appeal to a broad audience, at least if it did not appear to belong in a junior high school science fair. Some key points from Steve Jobs' biography

  • Co-founding Apple: In 1976, Jobs co-founded Apple Computer, Inc. (later renamed Apple Inc.) along with Steve Wozniak and Ronald Wayne. They developed the Apple I and Apple II computers.
  • Departure from Apple: In 1985, Jobs was ousted from Apple after disagreements with the board of directors.
  • NeXT Inc.: Following his departure from Apple, Jobs founded NeXT Inc., a computer company that produced high-end workstations.
  • Pixar Animation Studios: In 1986, Jobs acquired the computer graphics division of Lucasfilm and turned it into Pixar Animation Studios. The company produced groundbreaking animated films like Toy Story, Finding Nemo, and The Incredibles.
  • Return to Apple: In 1997, Apple acquired NeXT Inc., and Steve Jobs returned to Apple as an advisor, eventually becoming the interim CEO.
  • iMac and Apple's Revival: Jobs played a pivotal role in the development of the iMac, which was a significant success and marked the beginning of Apple's revival.
  • iPhone: In 2007, Apple launched the iPhone, a game-changing smartphone that redefined the mobile industry and solidified Apple's position as a technology leader.
  • Legacy: Steve Jobs is remembered for his visionary leadership, design philosophy, and innovation-driven approach, which had a profound impact on the technology and entertainment industries.
